Redesigning Yale Houston’s Website

 
 

Company
Yale Houston Club

Time
1 Week

Role
Sole Designer

 
 

CHALLENGE

Redesign the Yale Houston Alumni website within the iModules CMS platform

The current website is not designed with users in mind. It contains a total of 28 web pages, with 18 that feature a paragraph or less of content. Features such as “Career” and “Discussion” forums were not used at all.

Additionally, some elements are not properly labeled. For example, the screenshot below shows “Yale Daily News” (the name of the undergraduate school newspaper), but contains photo albums.

INITIAL EXPLORATION

When alumni visit the site, they prioritize events, news, and networking.

Based on usability interviews with 3 Yale alumni, I categorized and ranked their goals:

Events - Information on alumni events and campus events including athletics 

News - Updates on alumni and campus news, and emergency bulletins 

Networking - alumni networking features, including ways to Search for Alumni, find out information about a classmate or other alum, and the ability to view alumni blogs

Professional Development - Information about how to use the school to get a better job 

Giving Back - A way to donate to the school and become engaged in a meaningful way

This was further validated through online resources, such as this one from oho.com

DESIGN PROCESS

I built a series of mockups and received sponsor approval.

As I worked with two sponsors, I moved from low to high-fidelity throughout our conversations. I wanted to make sure they agreed with my proposed sitemap and layout sketches before I designed visuals.

Based on their feedback, I redesigned some elements due to platform development constraints.

FINAL STATE

I streamlined the site flow to 9 webpages (a 78% reduction!)

By consolidating information and removing unused features, I pared the original site from 28 pages down to 9. The final design is on Figma (Click for Prototype!)
I built the mockup from Paper —> Photoshop —> XD —> Figma. You can also view image mockups below.
